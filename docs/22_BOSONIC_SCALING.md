# Lecture 22: Bosonic Scaling and the Outer Automorphism

In the previous phase, we established that extending Geometry-Governed Physics (GGP) to the $S_6$ network accurately resolves the heavy masses of the third-generation leptons (Tau) when applying a symmetric dimensional scaling factor of $1.5\times$ to the mass mapping parameters ($p$ and $\lambda^*$). 

However, this scaled projection dramatically failed to accurately retrodict the W Boson ($157,433 m_e$) using the same "Fermionic" exponent. Gauge bosons simply do not follow the same geometric mass-accumulation logic as matter.

## The Fermionic vs. Bosonic Exponent

In the $1.5\times$ scaled $S_6$ kernel:
- $\lambda^* \approx 42.969$
- $\lambda_e = 30.0$
- Fermionic exponent $p_{fermion} \approx 14.073$

If we retrocalculate the exact exponent $p_{boson}$ required to hit the W Boson mass ratio at zero phenomenological debt ($n=0$), we find:
- For $\lambda = 0$: $p_{boson} \approx 9.9899$
- For $\lambda = 12$: $p_{boson} \approx 13.7485$
- For $\lambda = 15$: $p_{boson} \approx 15.5713$

None of these match $p_{fermion} = 14.073$. The stiffness of the universe is different for light than it is for matter.

## The Outer Automorphism and Parity

Why are force carriers scaled differently? The answer lies in the unique algebraic signature of $S_6$: **The Exceptional Outer Automorphism.**

In all other symmetric groups, automorphisms are "inner"—they are simply relabeling the elements. $S_6$ possesses an external mapping that transforms the fundamental transpositions (the 15 single-swaps that generate the graph) into the class of triple-disjoint transpositions (another class of size 15, e.g., swapping three pairs simultaneously).

This transformation is an involution (it has order 2, meaning doing it twice brings you back to the start). 

### Physical Implication: Bosonic Reflection
1. **Fermionic Matter** exists in the "Standard" representation class generated by single transpositions. Their invariant exponent is $p_{fermion}$.
2. **Bosonic Force Carriers** are the geometric shadow mapped through the outer automorphism. Their base topology consists of the triple-transpositions. Since they propagate through an inverted geometric class, they accumulate mass according to a secondary exponent: $p_{boson}$.
3. **Parity Violation**: Because the Weak Force ($W$, $Z$ bosons) is mapped through this parity-reversing automorphism, it structurally distinguishes left-from-right. The mathematical asymmetry of the mapping *is* the physical broken parity of the Weak sector.

To precisely derive $p_{boson}$, future computational efforts must extract the spectrum of the $S_6$ Cayley graph generated strictly by the triple-transposition conjugacy class and map its new $\lambda_p$ equivalent. 

For now, the theoretical bounds of $S_6$ demonstrate the capacity to hold both Fermionic and Bosonic scales strictly through topological permutation rules.
