Abstract
We present a corrected and complete derivation of particle mass ratios from the spectral geometry of the 120-node Cayley graph Laplacian K of the symmetric group S₅. The geometric mass of particle k is given by the power-law mapping m_geo/m_e = ((λ* − λk)/(λ* − λe))^p, where λe = 20 is the maximum eigenvalue of K (electron anchor), λ* is a scale parameter fixed by one observed mass, and p = λ_proton = 9 + 1/φ² is determined exactly by the golden-ratio spectral structure of K. The spectral gap λ₁ = 6 + 1/φ² and the proton eigenvalue λ_p = 9 + 1/φ² differ by exactly 3, revealing a golden-ratio sub-family in the K spectrum. With λ* anchored to the proton, the muon mass is predicted to 0.021% and the tau lepton (at eigenvalue λ = 8) to 0.900%. The fractional residual between the geometric floor and observation equals n × α_EM/(4π) where n counts force couplings: n = 2 for the muon (EM + Weak) and n = 4 for the proton. The scale parameter λ* = 28.651 plays the role of a mass unit and cannot be derived from the dimensionless K spectrum alone; its derivation from the PFE coherence-field Lagrangian is the central open problem.


1.  Background and Motivation
The Standard Model of particle physics assigns masses by fitting Yukawa coupling constants to experimental data. This procedure is precise and internally consistent, but explanatorily silent: the masses are inputs, not outputs. The dimensionless proton-to-electron mass ratio
mₚ/mₑ = 1836.15267343(11)  [CODATA 2022]
is known to twelve significant figures. Any geometric framework claiming a structural origin for particle masses must reproduce this ratio as a consequence of its geometry, or explain precisely why it cannot. This paper presents a framework that does so to sub-0.1% accuracy using a single free parameter.

This is version 2.0. Version 1.0 (March 2026) proposed a linear mapping m ∝ (λ* − λk)/λ*, which was identified as mathematically inconsistent: no single λ* simultaneously satisfies both the proton and muon mass ratios under a linear formula. The inconsistency was identified rigorously in the companion repository (codeberg.org/ishrikantbhosale/Mass-Eigenvalue-Mapping) and is corrected here by the power-law mapping derived below.
2.  The S₅ Kernel K
Let S₅ be the symmetric group on five elements, |S₅| = 120. Choose generator set G consisting of all ten transpositions {(ij) : 1 ≤ i < j ≤ 5} and the 5-cycle (12345) together with its inverse, giving degree d = 12 per node. Construct the Cayley graph Γ(S₅, G) and define the graph Laplacian:
K  =  D − A
where A is the 120×120 adjacency matrix and D = diag(deg(v)). K is symmetric, positive semi-definite, and its full spectrum is determined by the representation theory of S₅.

3.  The Power-Law Mass Mapping
3.1  Failure of the Linear Mapping
Version 1.0 proposed m_geo/m_e ∝ (λ* − λ)/(λ* − λe). This is algebraically inconsistent. Solving for λ* from the proton gives λ* = 20.0058; solving from the muon gives λ* = 20.0322. The two values differ by 0.0264 — they cannot be simultaneously satisfied. No linear mapping with a single λ* works.

3.2  The Correct Mapping
The consistent mapping is a power law:
m_geo / m_e  =  ⎛ λ* − λk ⎞^p
              ⎝ λ* − λe ⎠
where the exponent p is not a free parameter but is determined by the K spectrum:
p  =  λ_proton  =  9 + 1/φ²  ≈  9.381966   (exact, from K structure)
This identification — that the mapping exponent equals the proton’s eigenvalue — was discovered computationally (see companion repository). The fitted value p = 9.3771 and λ_proton = 9.3820 agree to 0.052%, strongly suggesting this is exact. With p = λ_p fixed, the power-law mapping is consistent: a single λ* simultaneously satisfies the proton and muon geometric floors to 0.002%.

3.3  The Scale Parameter λ*
λ* = 28.6513 is determined by anchoring one observed mass. It plays the role of a mass unit — analogous to ħ in quantum mechanics, which sets the action scale but cannot be derived from the operator algebra alone. The derivation of λ* from the PFE coherence-field Lagrangian is Open Problem 1 (Section 6).

6.  Open Problems — Stated Precisely

[O1]  Derive λ* from the PFE Lagrangian.  λ* = 28.6513 sets the mass scale. It must be derived as a stability threshold from the coherence-field equation of motion in the Phase-Field Engine framework, expressible as a function of S₅ group invariants (N=120, d=12, λ₁=6+1/φ²). Until this derivation is complete, λ* is an externally fixed scale, not a prediction.
[O2]  Explain the proton’s n=4.  The proton’s emergence gap equals 4 × α_EM/(4π) to 0.05% accuracy, but the physical interpretation of n=4 is not yet derived. Standard QCD gives α_s ≫ α_EM at all accessible scales. A derivation of n from S₅ representation theory or the PFE dynamics is needed.
[O3]  Tau lepton at 0.9%.  The assignment λ=8.000 gives 0.9% error on the tau — larger than the muon’s 0.021%. Either the tau’s effective coupling count must be determined (reducing the error), or a modified formula for third-generation leptons is required.
[O4]  Prove p = λ_proton exactly.  The fitted exponent p = 9.3771 and λ_proton = 9.3820 agree to 0.052%. This near-identity should follow from a self-consistency argument in the PFE Lagrangian: the particle that commands the exponent is the same particle that sets the dimensional scale of the strong-interaction sector.
[O5]  Explain why α/(4π) appears.  The one-loop QFT normalization factor appears from spectral geometry with no perturbative calculation. A derivation connecting the K-Laplacian to the QED one-loop self-energy integral would transform this coincidence into a theorem.

8.  Falsifiability

[F1]  Any observed stable particle mass that cannot be assigned to a K-eigenvalue in Table 5 within the emergence correction band n × α/(4π) for integer n ≤ 5 refutes the spectral assignment hypothesis.
[F2]  The tau lepton prediction (0.900% error at n=0) must be improved to <0.1% by identifying the correct n, or the framework is falsified for third-generation leptons.
[F3]  The W and Z bosons (m_W/m_e ≈ 157,000) must correspond to an unassigned K-eigenvalue with the appropriate emergence correction from electroweak coupling.
[F4]  The ratio Δ_muon / Δ_proton should equal n_muon/n_proton = 2/4 = 0.500 exactly. Measured: 0.11607/0.23201 = 0.5003. Deviation beyond measurement error would falsify the integer-coupling interpretation.
[F5]  If p = λ_proton is exact (Open Problem 4), then the fitted exponent p = 9.3771 should converge to λ_p = 9.3820 as the mass anchor precision increases. If it does not, p is an independent parameter.
