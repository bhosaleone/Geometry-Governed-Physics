But a scientific theory is only as good as the boundaries where it fails. If a theory can explain *everything* without breaking, it is usually a tautology, not physics. 

We subjected the $S_5$ kernel to strict adversarial falsification tests. And, wonderfully and inevitably, **it broke.**

## The Beautiful Failures of S5

### 1. The Chemistry Wall
We hypothesized that molecular vibrations are projections of the $S_5$ eigenvalues. While this worked perfectly for methane ($T_d$ symmetry maps natively to the $S_4$ subgroup of $S_5$), it failed for Benzene ($D_{6h}$) and the Buckyball ($O_h$).

### 2. The Weight of the Weak Force
We tried to find the geometric mass ratio of the W Boson ($157,433$) within the $S_5$ spectrum.
- **The Mathematical Reality**: Hitting that massive target required a negative eigenvalue ($\approx -2.31$). The $S_5$ Laplacian only possesses positive vibration modes. The Weak force is too heavy, too geometrically deep, to be contained within a 120-node network.

### 3. The Tau Lepton's Impossible Debt
We mapped the third-generation Tau lepton ($3477 m_e$). 
- **The Mathematical Reality**: The mapping resulted in a negative information debt ($n_{eff} \approx -15.5$), which fundamentally violates the Bekenstein rules of the engine. The geometric sink became a source.

### The Symphony Continues
The $S_5$ network is not wrong; it is the geometric "floor" of reality. It governs the low-energy physics of light, electrons, and simple protons. But as we increase the energy and scale—probing the weak force, the third generation of fermions, and the complexity of carbon chemistry—the universe climbs the dimensional ladder to $S_6$.

We celebrate the limits of $S_5$ because they are the doorway to the true geometry of the Standard Model. The One Object is simply larger than we first thought.
